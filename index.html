What is NCA & what went wrong?

New Customer Acquisition (NCA) mode tells Google to prioritize finding first-time buyers. We had it set to "bid only for new customers" — the most restrictive version.

The problem: Google identifies new customers using cookies. Users on iOS, Safari, and Firefox block third-party cookies by default — so Google couldn't classify them and excluded them from auctions entirely.

This silently blocked a massive chunk of eligible traffic, causing Brand Search spend to tank. Setting removed Mar 17. Brand recovered within a week.

The Renaldo Chapter — Critical Context

Renaldo was a fractional GM who appeared on calls in early March without introduction. He pushed to cut Thesis to $700/day (brand only) after seeing high in-platform CPAs on campaigns that were only 3 weeks old — without accounting for conversion lag or Google vs. Meta attribution differences. Pearmill pushed back with a formal budget proposal, but we were forced to comply with significant cuts. The result: NB campaigns were paused, the NCA setting simultaneously blocked iOS/Safari users from auctions, and the brand holdout test removed 50% of geo coverage — all at once. Brand performance cratered. The March 18 call confirmed Thesis was brand-only. Renaldo stopped appearing in meeting notes after March and is not mentioned in any April, May, or June syncs.

Reporting Highlights

Key Learnings from the Account

Note: Both accounts carry a 5–7 day conversion lag — every week's reported numbers look worse than reality. We track "projected" CPA alongside reported to account for this. It's the #1 source of Matt's weekly anxiety.

Thesis

  • Brand is reliable, non-brand is the growth bet. Brand Search has been the efficiency anchor throughout — every soft week traces back to NB or DemandGen volatility, not Brand.
  • NB Shopping is the strongest NB signal we've found. The best week: +120% spend with only +10% CPA increase. We keep trying to replicate it.
  • NB Broad and NB Exact behave differently. Exact scales predictably; Broad is lumpier. A tROAS change on May 28 caused conversions to drop -29% in one week — useful data on how sensitive it is.
  • Brand Shopping is a recurring soft spot. CPC competition, ROAS degrading, query leakage. We've been doing daily search term exclusions to manage it.
  • Stress Reset underperformed during Memorial Day. Conversions fell -42% even as spend rose. May be a landing page or demand issue — worth watching.

Stasis

  • Stasis is the harder account to scale efficiently. NB CPAs have consistently run above the $150 internal target. DemandGen's long conversion lag makes weekly reads unreliable.
  • The tROAS guardrails work. When we tightened tROAS on NB in mid-May, NB CPA improved -22% the following week. The tension is that tightening slows delivery, which Matt notices immediately.
  • Brand Shopping is fighting competitive pressure. Brand Shopping CPCs roughly doubled in May vs. prior period. This is a market dynamic, not something we control — important framing for any cost conversation.
  • AI Max is the most encouraging recent signal. Enabled late May on NB Broad — within one week Stasis NB Broad dropped below the $150 CPA target for the first time.
  • DemandGen is an upper-funnel play that requires patience. The conversion window is long enough that weekly reporting always makes it look inefficient.

Cross-Account Patterns

Conversion lag is the #1 reporting confusion driver

Every week we show both reported and projected CPA because the gap can be 20–30%+. Matt sees the reported number and reacts — this is the root cause of most tension on calls.

Incrementality tests running the whole time

The brand search geo holdout (50% of traffic excluded) suppressed Brand volume for weeks, making Brand look soft when it wasn't. That test just ended — Brand should normalize now.

Product disapprovals have been an operational headache

Multiple weeks flagged disapproval events that suppressed Shopping delivery. Some resolved quickly; others required case escalation with Google's policy team.

PMAX Shopping is the newest chapter

Just launched for both accounts in early June — both campaigns still in learning mode, contributing to short-term volatility. First clean read in 2–3 weeks.
